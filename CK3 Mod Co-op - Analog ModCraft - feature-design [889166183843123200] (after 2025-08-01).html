Hi Im Me And I Exist 9/3/2025 6:54 PM
From a historical perspective, imperial claimnants rarely appeared out of nowhere, they usually needed to establish a powerbase before going for the jugular. This is represented by factions and the need to first pull off a Claim Throne scheme, or by needing friends to pull off a Depose scheme. Caligula wasn't immediately overthrown, after all. Additionally, new emperors inherit the wars, including faction wars, of their predecessors. I do understand you want to create more chaotic succession situations like seen in the Crisis of the 3rd Century or the period following Basil II's accession, but like KeizerHarm pointed out mechanics already do a pretty decent job representing this aspect of Rome, the wrost sin really just being that peasant emperors have to go through the whole process of being an adventurer and buying an estate and can't just go from zero to hero within Constantinople like Justin I or Basil I. I think a better approach to your idea might be to simply increase AI Boldness in the ERE so they are more likely to start factions, perhaps tied to the Palace Politics tradition?
