For anyone interested, I made a fork of Buckzor's fork of the Paradox Import/Export addon for Blender to add the ability to transfer animation data from matching bones from another existing animation (including older animations) as well as the ability to only transfer animation data for select bones. Additionally I added a few quality of life additions, like the option to always create a rig when importing a mesh (meaning it won't skip importing the rig as sometimes happens when a existing matching rig is present), the option to apply animations to the currently selected rig only (rather than just to the initial rig that was imported), and the ability to auto-rotate ground joint of a animation when importing. You can find it here: https://github.com/Nerdman3000/io_pdx_mesh Some video examples from tests I was doing for reference: https://drive.google.com/file/d/1qvpKefYf_-YhxAlSEfKi7MOVWUVGQdq_/view?usp=drive_link https://drive.google.com/file/d/1UBWoK2GD0mqR6H8eWpUhuRCB2-NxMbUr/view?usp=drive_link https://drive.google.com/file/d/1CAooHO1DIGskTaWMUGgz61e3bJAYuUzQ/view?usp=drive_link https://drive.google.com/file/d/1eCJwFK2jjIFMXIBcaXG3hOl1BtGhZGgz/view?usp=sharing
